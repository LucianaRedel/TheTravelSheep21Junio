Argentina

Let's discover a little bit about this beautiful country: Argentina!

I am a native Argentinian citizen, and I can tell you we might have the richest country in the world in terms of nature and diversity: we have a vast territory with so many different landscapes, from mountains to desserts, waterfalls, green fields, jungle, glacier, and beaches. In winter, we have cold weather and snow in the south of the country, and warm sunny days in the north. Nature is beautiful and I am sure any place you choose to visit will take your breath away!


North of Argentina: Salta & Jujuy

There are so many beautiful places to visit in these two provinces, and if you want to get to know it all, you will need to spend at least ten days around there to enjoy it and get the best out of your trip. There are basically two ways of touring around, one is choosing one of the so many tour company options, and the other one, is renting a car and doing it by your own. It will depend on your budget and on your personal preferences and needs. However, you will also need to take into consideration that some routes going up are almost desserts and without internet signal, like the one to get to San Martin de los Cobres to ride the Tren de las nubes.

Some of the "do not dare to miss places" are:


Argentinian salt flats it is the third largest salt flats in the world. The landscape is amazing and completely natural. There are not any restaurants or facilities around, and you should also take into consideration that the reflection of the sun is blinding, and you will struggle to see much without sunglasses

Humahuaca gorge a world heritage site, located in the Province of Jujuy, is a narrow and arid mountainous valley, flanked by the high plateau of the Puna and the eastern wooded areas and follows the line of a major cultural route, the Camino Inca, along the spectacular valley of the Rio Grande. The Gorge is 2939 meters above ocean level, reason why it is recommended to walk with slowness, to avoid the unpleasant symptoms of the “apunamiento”, a malaise caused by the diminution of oxygen. The thermal amplitude is very vast: 38ºC by day and -10º C at night. Anticipate this when deciding what clothes you will take

Tren de las Nubes riding the train is a unique and special trip that combines its services in two sections: National Route 51 by bus, with photographic stops at panoramic points of places, to then embark on the magical experience of the “Tren de las nubes”, which manifests itself in all its splendor when arriving at the imposing viaduct in the foothills of the Andes. To avoid or diminishing altitude sickness, it will be necessary to acclimate, not agitate or making physical efforts, sleep well the day before, eat light and avoid alcoholic beverages

Cerro de los siete colores and Purmamarca The Hill of seven colors is one of the hills bordering the Purmamarca gorge. Its unique and breathtaking color range is the product of a complex geological history including marine sediments, lake, and river movements, and each one of them derive from different types of rocks: pink, white, red, green, yellow, brown, and purple. The small town of Purmamarca is situated at the bottom of the hills, the town has a lot of artisan stands selling all kinds of tapestries, hand-knitted clothing, artwork, sculptures, pottery, etc. It's said that the colors of the hill to be the most beautiful during the first 45 minutes after dawn

Cafayate it is a town located at the central zone of the Valles Calchaquies, and it is an important center to exploring the valleys and the wine yards of this area, where the most characteristic type of wine cultivated in the area is torrontes, and most wine cellars around town host free guided tours

Take also a day to tour around the cities of Salta and Jujuy, enjoy they restaurants, try the local food, visit the museums, walk along their parks and have fun discovering all their beauties


North of Argentina: Misiones & Iguazu waterfalls

Iguazu waterfall is one of the seven natural wonders of the world and one of the most imposing natural attractions on Earth. It is located in the north of the province of Misiones. The recommendation would be to stay a couple of days to get to know the most interesting places around and have some adventures in the jungle

Some things to do here:

1. Walking on the footbridge, from the visitor’s center, you have two circuits to follow, the upper path and the lower path. Both are different but worth it, just walk around and enjoy the panoramic views in an unforgettable experience

2. Have an adventure discovering one of the walking paths through the dense jungle, where live almost 500 species of mammals and an immense variety of reptiles, fish, insects, and butterflies live in this ecosystem

3. Enjoying a nautical trip along the Iguazú river is a different way to get to know the jungle with a humid subtropical climate that borders the rivers coasts with its lush foliage

4. National Park Salto Encantado, it ocuppies an area of almost 14 thousand hectares. There had been many legends about this place and its magical, religious and even extraordinay attributes. It is perfect for adventure travellers and specially for those who enjoy hiking


South of Argentina: San Martin de los Andes, San Carlos de Bariloche & Villa La Angostura

The season when you chose to travel here, winter or summer, it will depend on what you want to do and on your personal preferences, in spring and summer the landscape is green and full of flowers and colors, and in winter you will find snow everywhere and short days, since it gets dark early in the afternoon. From my opinion, visiting in summer and visiting in winter are both worth to travel.

An important advice, just keep in mind that telephone signal is lost during most of the road trip around these places, is you venture by your own, take a printed map, or a previously downloaded one with you just in case

San Martin de los Andes Is a town in northwest Patagonia, Argentina. It is known as a gateway to the forested Lanín National Park, home to the Lanín Volcano and diverse wildlife including guanacos and pumas. The town lies on the banks of Lake Lácar, one of the park’s many glacial lakes, and has a boat pier and a sandy beach.

Some things to do here:

a. Full day trip around the famous and beautiful road of 7 lakes

b. Full day trip to Lanin Volcano, Huenchulafquen lake and Junin de los Andes

c. Half day tour to Hua Hum and Chacin waterfall

d. Half day tour to Chapelco hill and visit to a Mapuche community

e. Full day trip to Quila Quina beach by boat

f. Spend a day touring around the city, which although small, has shops to visit and great places to eat and to enjoy


San Carlos de Bariloche It is located in Argentina's Río Negro province, is a city surrounded by thousand-year-old forests, mountains covered in snow and crystal clear lakes. It is quintessentially Patagonian. A city that is home to excellence and surrounded by some of the country's most beautiful natural spots.

What to do here?

a. Lake excursion to Victoria Island and Arrayanes forest

b. Cerro Catedral, whether in summer or in winter, there are activities such as skiing or hiking and biking

c. Full day excursion to Cerro Tronador, this is the highest mountain in the National Park Nahuel Huapi

d. Lake Nahuel Huapi by boat

Villa La Angostura It is located in the south of Neuquén province on the northwest shore of Nahuel Huapi Lake. Surrounded by lakes, forests, and mountains, it is without any doubt, the most beautiful city in the mountains and it worth your staying there for a few days. I dare to say that here, you just need to prepare to enjoy yourself touring around. Another interesting fact about this city is that is one of the most famous regions in the world for its excellent conditions for fishing: salmon, trout, king fish, catfish and piuyen.


South of Argentina: Usuahia & Calafate (Perito Moreno Glacier)

Usuahia It is known as the southernmost city in the world, is the capital of the Tierra del Fuego province that includes the Argentine arctic islands and the Islas Malvinas. It is a mystical land located on the bay of the largest island of the province

Some recommendations about this destination

a. Penguin watching tour by boat

b. Tour to Tierra del Fuego national park

c. Beagle channel and sea wolves island by boat

d. Lake Escondido tour

e. Esmeralda lagoon hiking tour

f. Vinciguerra glacier day tour

g. Tren del fin del mundo, short trip on this exceptional train connecting the known as end of the world train station to what it was the southernmost jail in the world

Calafate (Perito Moreno Glacier) Best known as the capital of glaciers is one of the most popular destinations in Argentina and Patagonia. It is located in the province of Santa Cruz and near to the Glaciers National Park, and it was named after a typical blueberry that grows in the area. This is a small city, easy to get around by foot, and it has beautiful views of Argentino Lake. The nearest airport counts with a good flight frequency, this is the easiest way to reach El Calafate. The city has around 47 glaciers around

What to do around here?

a. Perito Moreno glacier excursion, by boat or by foot, there are many options to enjoy the glacier, just pick the most convenient to you

b. Los tres lagos trekking tour


Buenos Aires city

Buenos Aires is Argentina’s capital city. Its center is the Plaza de Mayo, lined with stately 19th-century buildings including Casa Rosada, the iconic, balconied presidential palace. The city is a pulsating, passionate, cosmopolitan one. The combination of rich architectural and cultural heritage, modern creative energy, electric nightlife, unique traditions, a vibrant arts scene, extensive parks, and warm, friendly hosts makes it one of the world's most exciting capitals.

Some places to visit and "must do" tours

1. Jardin Japones

2. Teatro Colon

3. Enjoy a Tango show

4. Walk around Bosques de Palermo park, and visit El Rosedal (Roses's garden)

5. Visit Plaza de Mayo and Casa Rosada

6. Go to the most expensive district, Puerto Madero

7. Have a cup of coffee at the mythical Cafe Tortoni

8. Tour around the neighborhood of Caminito

Of course, these are only some places, that in my opinion, are worth visiting in Argentina. But there are so many other destinations in this beautiful country: Mendoza and its high quality wineries, Cordoba with its magnificent hills, Chubut and the whale approaching view experience, just to mention a few ones. So now that you have seen a little bit of what Argentina has to offer you, is up to you to select the date and to plan your trip!

Need more reasons to visit? Here are just a few more: You can dance the world's most romantic dance in its birthplace, experience sporting passion like never before, enjoy nightlife that goes on well into the morning, amaze yourself with the most beautiful natural landscapes of the world, and you will for sure eat like a king!


Travel information

Language Spanish

Currency Argentinian Peso (ARS)

Local weather Summer from December to March

Health Always travel with a medical insurance, this is very important

VISA and travel papers Before planning your trip, please find out if you need or not, any special documentation to enter the country

It will mainly depend on many things, such as your budget, the duration of your stay, the kind of experience you are looking for, if you are in a group or in a solo trip, the weather in your destination, the size of the city you are visiting, and so on.

You can choose among a Hotel, a Hostel, an apartment or even a room. Here are some tips to help you decide, take into consideration the comments of previous guest, search the reputation of your expected host or hotel, check on the neighborhood you are planning to stay at, find out traveling extra costs you could need to afford is you plan to stay in a faraway place, and in this case, you could maybe even consider renting a car and evaluate it cost. You should also review the booking and payment terms of course, and consider the exchange rate. One last thing you might consider, it is if this is your first time there or if you are returning to an old destination

If you visit the city of Buenos Aires, some good neighborhoods you can choose are Palermo, Belgrano and Recoleta; the location is good and safe enough.

However, while doing this evaluation, you may find that you lean to one destination even though the facts point to another. Remember, the final decision about the right travel destination for you is yours to make, no one else’s!

A trip to Argentina is almost always associated with eating and drinking and you certainly cannot leave the country without enjoying fantastic wines and food from Argentina, ideally at every meal. Argentinian gastronomy is the result of its mixed heritage which draws from Spanish, Italian and Portuguese colonization.

Walking around Buenos Aires you may feel like you are among the bistros of Paris, or in a neighbourhood of Milan or Madrid. This multicultural, but also markedly European feel, is also reflected in the food.

What should you try here?

For breakfast: Dulce de leche, Mate, Alfajores, Medialunas and a mix of all sorts of Danish pastries, Ham and cheese toasted sandwich, Churros

For lunch or dinner: Asado, Empanadas, Choripan, Chimichurri sauce, Pizza, Faina, Milanesas, Matambre, Miga sandwiches (many tastes)

Some desserts: Chocotorta, Flan with dulce de leche, Dulce de leche pancake (panqueque)

Although Argentina is a beautiful country, there are some safety points to mention, especially if you are visiting big cities: take care of your belongings everywhere you go, do not venture by yourself to dangerous neighborhoods, try to avoid walking along holding your camera or your cellphone, do not be scared, but just being alert will surely help you to enjoy your experience there.
