Brazil

Let's watch a video to get inspired about this warm relaxing destination: Brazil!

With its unique cuisine, rich culture, some of the most beautiful beaches in the world, breathtaking waterfalls, and adventure for everyone’s liking, all you have to do is choose the perfect type of tourism for you and be amazed by the wonders of Brazil.

Rio de Janeiro & Sao Paulo

Rio de Janeiro Is a huge seaside city in Brazil, famed for its Copacabana and Ipanema beaches, Christ Redeemer statue atop Mount Corcovado and for Sugarloaf Mountain, a granite peak with cable cars to its summit. The city is also known for its sprawling favelas (shanty towns). Its raucous Carnaval festival, featuring parade floats, flamboyant costumes, and samba dancers, is considered the world’s largest.

Experience the rhythm of this amazing city by visiting the various beaches, museums, art galleries, mountains, parks and last but certainly not the least the famous Rio Carnivals.

Things to do in Rio

1. Corcovado mountain and Christ Redeemer statue

2. Sugar Loaf Mountain

3. Tijuca rain forest tour

4. Rio de Janeiro walking tour

5. Maracana stadium

6. Rocinha favela walking tour (please take into consideration that, although this is a guided tour, it is still a shanty town, with all what that means. On the hand, it requires lots of stairs and walking on uneven and tight surfaces)

7. Paraty island, which combines history, breathtaking landscapes, extraordinary natural beauty and a unique architectural ensemble

8. Of course, go to the beach!

Buzios Located about 170 km from the city of Rio de Janeiro, the city and its stunning beaches are an excellent option for visitors who want to enjoy the state coast and all of its beauty. The coastline, which contains many paradise beaches with crystal-clear waters, make Búzios a very popular destination. In addition to having great infrastructure that includes hotels, bars, restaurants, and plenty of leisure activities, the city stands out for the beauty of its beaches. The city has more than 20 beaches that will please any kind of tourist. There are, for example, beaches that are beautiful enough to be in the movies, quiet beaches, busy beaches, and beaches that are perfect to practice water sports and other activities.

Sao Paulo Home to 20 million fiercely proud paulistanos (as residents are known), all of whom will happily tell you at length to you how they would never live elsewhere. The city is Brazil’s vibrant financial center, is among the world's most populous cities, with numerous cultural institutions and a rich architectural tradition.

Places to visit

1. Visit the Copan building, The building, with its serpentine facade and narrow brises soleil (permanent sunshades), is Sao Paulo most symbolic structure. There are over 1150 apartments in total, making it Brazil’s largest residential building

2. Mercado Municipal, wandering the various stalls here, sampling as you go and taking in the sights and sounds of everyday Paulistano moving and shaking, is one of the city’s great joys.

3. Campos do Jordao, Visit the highest city in Brazil and explore the city's natural and historical attractions with your local guide and admire the surrounding Serra da Mantiqueira mountains. Stop by the impressive Boa Vista Palace, summer home to the Governor of Sao Paulo and the Borboletário da cidade, where you'll taste locally brewed beer as you watch the butterflies flit by. Have lunch in a local restaurant (own expense), where you can try the regional specialties

4. Explore the city taking a walking tour


North of Brazil: best beaches

Fortaleza, Maragogi, Recife & Salvador

Containing a stunning coastline, the north area has beaches with completely different characteristics from each other. Some are located in busy neighborhoods with lots of bars and beach clubs on the waterfront, while others are very secluded and can only be reached through trails in the middle of the woods or by boat. And then there are those beaches that are perfect to practice water sports including, among others, windsurfing, surfing, kitesurfing, and diving.

Maragogi, Is a resort town on the Atlantic coast in eastern Brazil. It is known for its long beaches such as Burgalhau, near the mangrove-lined Dos Paus River. Offshore coral reefs and sandbars have created a shallow lagoon, which acts as a natural pool. South of Maragogi is São Bento, a fishing village with a palm-edged beach. The coast here is part of the Coral Coast protected area, which is rich in flora and fauna.

Recife, Welcoming and full of life, the city of Recife has beautiful beaches with clear blue waters. But it is not only natural beauty that makes it so special. The city’s historic monuments, local music, and the people’s hospitality also help in making it so attractive.

The harmonious contrast between the monuments in Recife Antigo (the old town) and the modern buildings in the business center is just one of the city’s attractions. Visitors cannot resist the stunning local beaches and their natural pools with warm water.

There is a true paradise only 50 km from the city of Recife, a place where visitors will find white-sand beaches and crystal-clear waters surrounded by coconut trees. Porto de Galinhas, this in an incredible region renowned for its natural beauty.

Porto de Galinhas, Is home to so many charming attractions that it has become one of the main tourist centers in the state of Pernambuco. The main attractions in the region include natural pools with clear warm water, white-sand beaches, estuaries, coconut trees, and mangroves. Located in the municipality of Ipojuca, the beach is the ideal destination for tourists looking for warm weather as well as peace and quiet.

The region has options for all kinds of tourists, and accommodations include luxurious resorts, hotels of different categories, and charming rustic inns. There are plenty of activities to choose from, but one of the favorites among visitors is a walk along the Porto de Galinhas beach promenade. Visitors can relax and recharge their batteries while enjoying the busy local businesses.

Salvador Has lots of beautiful beaches, each one with different characteristics. Local beaches are great for several types of water activities, such as surfing, bodyboarding, kitesurfing, windsurfing, and stand up paddling. The city’s waterfront is lively both during the day and at night. There are always plenty of people walking, jogging, cycling, rollerblading, skateboarding, and enjoying the local bars and kiosks.

Natal The capital city in the state of Rio Grande do Norte, is a destination whose natural beauty countless tourists come to enjoy every year. In addition to the beautiful landscapes scattered all around the entire city, the gorgeous local beaches also stand out. Since Natal is such a charming city, its beaches could not be any different! The vibrant blue-green color of the sea contrasts beautifully with the native vegetation and with the nearby golden dunes. You may think it’s a dream but it’s totally real. South of Brazil: best beaches

Florianopolis & Camboriu

Those who visit the South Region of Brazil will find it unforgettable. Comprising 3 states, Paraná, Santa Catarina, and Rio Grande do Sul, the region has delightful destinations. Visitors will be able to experience places such as the amazing Iguaçu National Park, on the UNESCO World Natural Heritage List, as well as charming mountain destinations such as the highlands in Rio Grande do Sul and Santa Catarina.

Florianopolis, Popularly known as the Magic Island, is the capital city in Santa Catarina State. Visitors will fall in love with its charm and natural beauty. It comprises two parts: the island just off the coast and a small peninsula on the continent. They are joined by the beautiful Hercílio Luz Bridge, one of the main landmarks in the city. Containing around 100 beaches, the region is perfect to practice water sports such as surfing, kite surfing, and sailing. However, unlike other Brazilian cities, temperatures tend to drop during the winter season (June to August). Therefore, brace yourself for colder waters.

Camboriu, From gleaming skyscrapers to a steaming-hot beach scene, this city can feel like Rio’s younger sibling. Locals enjoy the same wild nightlife, great restaurants, and backdrop of rolling green slopes, while the hilltop Christo Luz statue even mimic's Rio's massive Christ the Redeemer.

Brazil is blessed with a coastline that stretches for more than 7,000 kilometers, and that translates into beautiful beaches all over the country. In each of them, you will be able to experience the sun, energy, beauty, and joy of Brazilians. If your dream trip involves sunshine, beaches, and having your feet in the sand, Brazil has countless options for you.

Brigadeiro This are balls of tuffles made with condensed milk and covered in chocolate sprinkles

Canjica, Is a bowl of whole pieces of white corn mixed with milk, a splash of coconut milk and condensed milk to make a creamy, enviable dessert with sprinkles of cinnamon on top.

Açaí, Try it plain as sorbet, or ask for banana, strawberry or granola to be added in to take it to a whole new level of tastiness

Pão de Queijo, This is so much more than just cheese bread. Made with cassava flour so it is totally gluten-free, these little puffs are crispy on the outside, squidgy on the inside and filled with cheesy flavor

Beijinho de Coco, The name literally translates to little kiss of coconut, which fits perfectly with this mouth-sized dessert. It’s made with condensed milk and butter mixed together before being rolled up into little balls and covered in coconut flakes

Farofa, Is unique to Brazil and its taste and significance is hard to understand without actually trying it. It is basically fried tapioca flour mixed with small pieces of fried bacon and served alongside rice and beans

Feijoada, Is one of the Brazilian main dishes you can find the most and is eaten in every corner of the country. It’s a pot of black beans cooked with chunks of meat, although the truly traditional feijoadas cook it with pig’s ears, trotters and other parts. It’s served with fried kale mixed with bacon bits, rice, farofa (here, farofa works so well to absorb those last bits of bean sauce) and a slice of orange to flavor.

Prawn skewer, Fresh prawns on a skewer is the number one food to be spotted on the beaches in Brazil

Mandioca frita, Fried cassava is a popular choice in Brazil as an alternative to chips. They’re chunky, crispy and delicious, not to mention a healthier option than normal french fries.

Beer, All over Brazil, most of the places that serve beer will do so in smaller glasses that are usually smaller than the ½ pint glasses. The idea behind this makes sense as most areas in Brazil experience heat that is extreme, and the larger glasses warm up faster

Caipirinha, Is Brazil's national cocktail, made with cachaça, sugar, and lime. The drink is prepared by mixing the fruit and the sugar together, then adding the liquor. This can be made in a single large glass to be shared among people, or in a larger jar, from which it is served in individual glasses.

Brazil is somewhat safe, but you should take precaution on the streets of major cities, and after dark. Be wary of pickpockets and bag snatching and keep your valuables safely by your side. Never carry all your money in one place or leave your valuables in plain sight in a car or at a beach when swimming.

When in Brazil always wait for every car to stop completely before you decide to cross a road, regardless of whether there is a green man telling you are allowed to cross.

Subways in the main cities offer carriages for women only, yet this rule is only applicable during the peak hours from 6am to 9 am and 5 pm to 8 pm from Monday to Friday. If men enter one of these carriages, they could face a fine of as much as R$1,000 which translates into $320USD. Outside the peak hours, both women and men are permitted to use these carriages.

It’s often assumed that South America is a budget-friendly place to visit and in general, this is an accurate assumption. However, a few of the main cities like Rio de Janeiro and São Paulo can be costly, especially in wealthier neighborhoods like Leblon and Ipanema which are also some of the top tourist areas.

Drinking tap water in Brazil is safe but due to its treatment process, the taste is not very good. Therefore, most Brazilians choose to drink bottled water instead of tap water.
